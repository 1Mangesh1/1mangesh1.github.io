## What I'm up to right now

April was the month of trying things and watching most of them not work. The theme was experiments — short, scrappy, often broken. I wanted to stop reading about voice agents and actually build one. Turns out the gap between "I understand the API" and "this thing feels good to talk to" is enormous.

## Voice AI rabbit hole

Spent a chunk of April deep in voice. Deepgram for streaming STT, Whisper for batch transcription, playing with realtime APIs and TTS providers. The interesting problem isn't the model — it's the pipeline. Latency budget gets eaten alive by network, VAD, turn-taking, and the LLM round-trip. A 200ms speech-to-speech target sounds reasonable until you actually measure where the time goes.

A few things that surprised me:

- Endpointing is harder than transcription. Knowing when the user stopped talking is the actual product.
- Barge-in support changes the whole architecture. You can't bolt it on later.
- Whisper is great for batch, not great for live. Streaming STT is a different sport.
- TTS quality has gotten weirdly good. Latency is the bottleneck, not naturalness.

## Failed experiments

Most of what I built in April did not ship. A voice-driven note-taker that lost context every 30 seconds. An agent that confidently routed to the wrong tool four times in a row. A streaming pipeline that worked locally and fell apart on a flaky connection. Useful failures — each one taught me which assumption was wrong.

## Performance tuning

Kept chipping at site performance on the side. Smaller bundles, fewer round trips, killing things that nobody used. Boring work, real wins. The kind of changes that don't show up in screenshots but make the site feel different.

## What I'm learning

Voice changes how you think about latency, state, and error recovery. Text agents can stall for two seconds and nobody notices. Voice agents stall for 400ms and the conversation breaks. Forces a different kind of engineering discipline.

Also: ship the experiment even if it's bad. The lessons are in the broken version.
