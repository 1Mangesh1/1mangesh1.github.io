## What I'm up to right now

May is April, continued. Still deep in voice agents — but now with better tools. April was raw APIs and duct tape. May is frameworks that handle the boring parts so I can focus on the parts that actually matter.

## LiveKit

Found LiveKit and it changed the shape of the problem. WebRTC infra, turn detection, barge-in, transport — all the plumbing I was hand-rolling in April is just there. The Agents framework gives you a sane way to wire STT → LLM → TTS without reinventing the pipeline. Going to spend May porting my broken April experiments onto it and seeing which ones actually work when the infra stops fighting me.

## Tavus

Tavus is the other rabbit hole. AI video avatars that can hold a conversation in real time. Different problem space than voice-only — now you have lip sync, expression, and a face that has to feel non-creepy. Mostly experimenting to see what the latency and quality ceiling looks like. Not sure where it lands yet, but the demos are uncanny in a good way.

## Webhooks and alerts

The other thread this month: webhooks. Building a small alerting system and learning the hard way that webhooks are easy to send and hard to do well. Signed payloads, retries with backoff, idempotency keys, dead-letter queues, replay tooling. The "send a POST request" version takes ten minutes. The "won't fall over in production" version takes weeks.

A few things I'm chewing on:

- HMAC signatures + timestamp tolerance to prevent replay
- Exponential backoff with jitter, capped retries, then DLQ
- Idempotency on the receiver side — webhooks WILL be delivered twice
- Observability: every event needs a trace, every failure needs a reason

## Experiments still failing

Carrying forward the April rule: ship the bad version. Several voice + avatar experiments are limping along. A few work well enough to demo. Most don't. Still learning more from the broken ones.

## What I'm learning

Frameworks like LiveKit are a forcing function — they push you toward the right architecture by making the wrong one annoying. Same with webhook discipline: once you've debugged one missed delivery in production, you stop cutting corners.

Voice + video + reliable async events is starting to feel like the stack for the next thing I want to build.
