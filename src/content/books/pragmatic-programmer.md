A timeless classic that shaped how I think about software development. The 20th anniversary edition updates the original wisdom for modern contexts.

## Key Takeaways

- **DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself)** - Every piece of knowledge must have a single, unambiguous representation
- **Orthogonality** - Keep components independent and interchangeable
- **Tracer Bullets** - Build end-to-end slices to validate architecture early
- **Broken Windows** - Fix small issues before they compound
- **Rubber Duck Debugging** - Explain your problem to find the solution

## Favorite Quotes

> "Care about your craft. Why spend your life developing software unless you care about doing it well?"

> "Don't live with broken windows."

## Who Should Read This

Every developer, regardless of experience level. Beginners learn principles that prevent bad habits; seniors get reminded of fundamentals they may have forgotten.
